Establish a routine

Children thrive on routine, so it’s important to establish a daily routine that includes regular meal times, nap times, and bedtimes. This will help your child feel secure and develop good habits.

Provide a safe environment

Make sure your home is childproofed and free of hazards. Keep cleaning supplies, medications, and other dangerous items out of reach. Install safety gates at the top and bottom of stairs, and use outlet covers to prevent electrical shocks.

Encourage healthy habits

Encourage your child to eat a healthy diet, get plenty of exercise, and practice good hygiene. Teach them to wash their hands frequently, cover their mouth when they cough or sneeze, and brush their teeth twice a day.

Spend quality time together

Spend time playing with your child, reading to them, and engaging in other activities that promote bonding. This will help your child feel loved and valued.

Be patient and understanding

Children can be challenging at times, but it’s important to remain patient and understanding. Try to see things from your child’s perspective, and respond to their needs with empathy and kindness.
